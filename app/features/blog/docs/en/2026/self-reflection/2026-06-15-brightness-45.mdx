## The Old Standard

I used to think life needed to be a bit brighter.

Even if not all the time,  
I think I felt that a brightness level around 70 or 80 was what made a state meaningful.  
If I wasn't that joyful, that happy, that full of conviction, something felt missing.

Living at a slightly lower brightness sometimes felt trivial.  
It seemed like a state I wasn't truly living in yet—something I would have to escape eventually.

But I feel a bit differently now.

Even if my baseline brightness is around 45, that might not necessarily be a bad thing.

## Familiar Low Light

I think I've lived quite accustomed to sadness and painful emotions.

If anyone remembers me as a bright and cheerful person,  
there were certainly moments when I genuinely felt at ease and enjoyed myself.  
But in some of those moments, I may have been trying a little to appear that way.

Not deep despair, but emotions that are a bit self-mocking and slightly negative.  
Not complete collapse into darkness, but a low ambient light that's always present somewhere.

Strangely, I sometimes feel stability in that kind of state.

It's not that I dislike being bright and joyful.  
On the contrary, I often long for those moments and wish they would last longer.

But somewhere in my heart, the slightly darker side feels more familiar.

"Ah, this is a feeling I know."  
"It hurts, but it's not unfamiliar."  
"In a way, this side feels a little more like me."

I have that kind of sense.

So even when I feel I'm moving toward joy and happiness,  
there are times when I suddenly feel pulled back toward familiar darkness.  
Almost like being drawn to a magnet.

And then, strangely, a sense rises that this side feels more like me.

I used to feel that as a flaw in myself.  
Why can't I stay in bright places for long?  
Why do I keep trying to return to something a bit lonely?

But now I don't necessarily think of it as a fated default value.

It might be an emotional habit built over a long time.  
It might be my mind trying to return to a familiar room.

That room isn't bright, but I've stayed there so long I know the way.  
So it's uncomfortable but predictable.

## 45 as a Baseline

These days I find myself thinking about the brightness of my life in numbers.

Maintaining a life as bright as 100 might not suit me.  
Of course moments at 70 or 80 are good. When they come, I'm glad.  
I also think I'd like them to happen often if possible.

But my life isn't a failure just because that doesn't continue.

Perhaps the most realistic and stable range for me is somewhere between 45 and 60.

A state that's a bit calm, with a slight shadow, not too lifted up but not collapsing either.  
A state where I can think, work, write, and look at myself to some degree.

At that level of brightness, I can live well enough.

The old me seemed to look down on 45 too much.  
I think I believed that life below 70 wasn't a proper life.

But now I know.

There's depth at 45.  
There's rhythm at 45.  
There's a temperature at which I can move in a way that feels like me.

What matters might not be maintaining 80 or above,  
but not staying below 40 for too long.

Below 40 feels different.  
A state where I might neglect my body, abandon what I need to do,  
cut off connection with people, even start self-blame.  
That's closer to a signal that I need to warm myself up again—not "my kind of darkness."

But the darkness at 45 is different.

There, I can look at myself calmly.  
I can focus on my work.  
And when good moments come, I can rise to 70 or 80.

## Not Clinging to Bright Moments

After a lot of time has passed,  
maybe it's because I've gradually stopped pushing myself so hard,  
or maybe I've grown a little numb—I don't know.

These days I feel I don't have to try so hard to hold on to bright moments.

If there's a day when my mood rises, I can treat it as a bonus and feel grateful.  
Today feels a bit lighter.  
Today my brightness rose to around 70.  
That's enough to accept.

And even if I return to around 50, I don't have to see that as failure.

I used to feel disappointed when bright emotions disappeared.  
It felt like being pushed back to my original place.

But now I want to think about it a bit differently.

I've simply returned near my baseline light.  
That place isn't necessarily bad.  
I can live there too.

I just need to make sure that place doesn't become a room that traps me.

## A Blessing Lately

Fortunately, these days I hardly ever drop below 40.

Even if I dip slightly, I don't seem to stay there long.  
And after a little time, I feel myself recovering to around 45 again.

I think that's a fairly big change.

It doesn't mean I've become always bright.  
It doesn't mean I've become always positive.

What's more important is that the strength to come back has definitely grown stronger.

Not collapsing completely even when shaken.  
Not making brief darkness my only home.  
Knowing the path back to my stable range a little better.

Perhaps that's the kind of life standard I need now.

Not a life of trying to become brighter,  
but a life of not abandoning myself when I start leaning toward the darker side.

## Learning What Brightness Works for Me

I still don't know for sure what living well means.

Everyone's standard is different, and the answer may change with the season.  
For someone, a life at brightness 80 might be a good life.  
For someone else, a life around 60 might be most stable.

Right now I'm probably someone a bit closer to between brightness 45 and 60.

When moments at 70 or 80 come now and then, that's wonderful.  
I want to cherish and enjoy those moments.

But it's also okay to return near 50 again.  
That might not be a fall, but something closer to my basic rhythm.

However, if I do go below 40,  
I want to watch over myself so I don't stay there long,  
and so I can return to 45–60 quickly.

I don't have to be a very bright person.  
I might be someone who moves more calmly, more deeply,  
more like myself in slightly darker places.

For now, that level of understanding is enough.

Knowing the brightness that fits me,  
not collapsing within that brightness,  
and not missing the brighter moments that visit from time to time.

Perhaps what I now think of as "living well" is close to that.  
Only now do I feel I'm starting to understand that a little.
