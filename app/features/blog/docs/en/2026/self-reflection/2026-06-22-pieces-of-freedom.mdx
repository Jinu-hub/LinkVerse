## Back Behind the Wheel After Two Months

Today I rented a car again for the first time in about two months.

The destination was an onsen tucked fairly deep in the mountains of Nara Prefecture.  
I drove for nearly five hours round trip.

At first I was a little tense.  
Every moment I pulled onto the road, I could feel that my body wasn't fully used to it yet.  
But after a little while, I felt like the sensation of being out in the world was coming back.

Watching the lane, matching speed, taking curves, waiting at signals, passing through narrow roads.  
Those ordinary movements gradually settled back into my body.

## Becoming Someone Who Could Drive Again

I actually got my driver's license in Korea a long time ago.  
But after coming to Japan, I hardly drove at all.  
Time passed like that, and before I knew it, driving had become something distant from my daily life.

Then last year, I felt that I couldn't put it off any longer.

The process of converting a Korean license to a Japanese one wasn't that complicated in itself.  
The problem was that too few people could have their foreign licenses converted to Japanese ones each day.  
The competition was fierce.

In the end, to make it inside the first-come cutoff line, I set up a camping chair near the licensing office the night before and stayed up all night.

Looking back now it's a little funny, but I was quite serious at the time.  
Becoming someone who could drive again.  
That's still somewhat true for me now, but especially then, it wasn't simply a matter of getting a license in my hands.

And that exhausting all-nighter has become a good memory now.  
I don't keep in touch with them anymore, but I still vividly remember the faces of the people who stayed up that night with me.

## The Feeling of Leaving Home Returning

This year was when I finally started practicing in earnest.

In February I went out four times. At first I only circled parking lots, then gradually ventured onto actual roads.  
In March, twice. My body already felt less unfamiliar than before.  
In April, once. Even then, the feeling that I could drive again was clear.  
And today, after about two months, I held the steering wheel again.

It had been nearly fifteen years since I last held a wheel, so I was tense at first.  
But today was a little different.  
I wouldn't say I felt completely confident, but something was clearly coming back.

My sense for driving was returning.

## What It Means to Expand the Freedom to Travel

The reason I started driving again isn't simply that I wanted to get behind the wheel.  
I wanted to expand my freedom to travel.

When a place I wanted to go came to mind, I didn't want to be someone who checked train schedules or bus routes first—  
I wanted to become someone who could move at my own rhythm.

Sometimes I picture myself taking off somewhere with just a laptop,  
sketching ideas or coding in an unfamiliar place.

Driving deep into the mountains of Nara today, I felt that this practice was more than just driving practice.

It is a way of gradually widening the radius of my life.  
A way of regaining the body and the senses to actually move when there is somewhere I want to go.  
And also a way of fitting together, one piece at a time, the puzzle pieces I need for the life I will build ahead.

## The Future I Already Know

There is a life I will build within the next two and a half years—or five at the longest.

> company dependence : NO  
> money worries : NO  
>
> time freedom : YES  
> location freedom : YES

This isn't just a vague wish.  
Inside me, it already feels close to a settled future.

I know I will become that way.  
And I know that someday I will complete the whole puzzle.

Of course, not every part of the process will be easy.  
Even now there is much I lack, hard days, moments of loneliness.

But strangely, those things don't all feel like evidence of failure.  
They feel more like things that belong inside the process of moving toward the future I've laid out.

## A Present Where the Future Can Stay

The present is a result of the past, but at the same time it is the place prepared for the future to begin.

If there is a future I want to reach, I need a present where that future can stay.  
That's why today matters.

Perhaps the future is the cause, and the present is the result.  
Because I already know the future that awaits, the me of today moves little by little.

Practicing driving again is like that too.  
On the surface it may look like nothing more than a day of renting a car and visiting an onsen.  
But to me it feels a little different.

This is a small practice toward location freedom.  
A way of widening the radius of my life.  
A way of making today—where the future I want can stay—just a little more solid.

## Being Someone Who Enjoys the Process

Living joyfully and happily doesn't always mean feeling upbeat all the time.

There are good days and hard days.  
Moments of gratitude and moments of loneliness.  
Days when the heart feels light, and days when it feels a little heavy.

If I can accept all of that and still feel grateful for small things—  
if I can quietly rejoice in a returning sensation, like driving along a mountain road as I did today—  
if I can keep the direction I'm heading in view and gradually fill my heart with richness—

I think I can say I'm happy even now.

I'm not yet living inside completed freedom.  
But I am in the present that is moving toward that freedom.

I am always fitting the puzzle pieces together like that.  
And I know I will complete the whole puzzle.

So I try not to rush, and to enjoy the process itself.

Today too, like that, I am unfolding the future with joy.
